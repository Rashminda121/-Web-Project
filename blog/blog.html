DOGS

A dog is a domestic mammal that belongs to the order Carnivora and family Canidae. Canis lupus familiaris is its scientific name. Dogs are linked to foxes and jackals and are a subspecies of the gray wolf. One of the two most common and well-liked domestic animals in the world is the dog.

A few significant dog facts

  • Dogs can read our emotions.
  • Dogs can see more than just black and white.
  • The tallest dog in the world is 44 inches tall.
  • Dogs don't sweat like we do.
  • Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
  • Along with their noses, their hearing is super sensitive.
  • Dogs have 18 muscles controlling their ears.
  • Meat, Chicken, beef, turkey are some food that dogs eat the most.

CATS

A domesticated animal is the cat. Felis cats is the scientific name for it. It is a little creature from the "Felidae" family. The only member of the family that has been domesticated is the cat. Tigers, panthers, and other animals are also included. Many people throughout the world love to pet cats because they are such cute creatures. Playing with them makes you feel less stressed and anxious.


Some vital information about cats


  • Cats are omnivorous.
  • cats eat vegetative items such as rice, milk, pulses, as well as fish, meat, birds, mice, etc.
  • Apart from being clever and sweet, cats are also skilful hunters.
  • Cats are also helpful to their owners as they protect the household from rats.
  • They have two mesmerizing eyes, two highly sensitive ears, four legs, whiskers under their nose and a long tail.

HORSES

Horses are domesticated, vegetarian animals. It is also quite advantageous. A horse has two eyes, a nose, two ears, a tail, and four feet. They have very strong yet very thin feet. They can sprint quickly and far thanks to it.


Significant facts on horses

  • Horses are in many colours. There are white, red, brown, black, grey horses and also sometimes they have a mixture of colours.
  • A horse lives up to 25 to 30 years.
  • Horses like living in grassy areas or field where they can eat the grass, leaves and all other types of greenery available.
  • The quality and quantity they eat daily also impact their health.
